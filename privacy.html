Privacy Stack.

Certified privacy nerd (self-appointed). These are the tools I've actually settled on — shared here because everyone deserves to know this exists.

no one is paying me for this (sadly). this is just what works for me.

Browser

Brave

Chromium-based browser with built-in shields. Blocks ads, trackers, and fingerprinting out of the box — no extensions needed. All your Chrome extensions still work.

brave.com Password Manager

Proton Pass

Open-source password manager from the Proton team — the same people behind ProtonMail. End-to-end encrypted, zero-knowledge, built in Switzerland under strong privacy laws.

proton.me/pass Two-Factor Auth

Proton Authenticator

Open-source TOTP app with end-to-end encrypted backups. Unlike Google Authenticator, you won't lose all your 2FA codes if you lose your phone. Also use Google Authenticator where needed.

proton.me/pass/authenticator DNS

Quad9

Free, privacy-focused DNS resolver (9.9.9.9) that blocks malicious domains at the DNS layer. Doesn't log your queries. Run by a Swiss non-profit — no commercial interest in your data.

quad9.net Network

Pi-hole

A DNS sinkhole running on a Raspberry Pi Zero W that blocks ads and trackers for every device on the home network. Your TV, phone, laptop — all covered without installing anything on them.

pi-hole.net Mobile

Phone Privacy

On iPhone 14 — App Tracking Transparency on, location set to "never" for most apps, ad personalisation off. On Android — DuckDuckGo's VPN blocks app-level trackers across every app on the device.

DDG App Tracking Protection
